Background

Recently swarm robotics has been gathering attention because swarm robots are more flexible and autonomous than single robots in many cases. The movement of swarm robots refer to the group behavior of ants and fish and so on. For example, the research group in Harvard University is trying to develop ‘thousand-robot swarm.’ In this system, more than 1000 robots form a programmed figure autonomously. Swarm robots can be useful in many fields, and one of the fields is the body. By administering the robots in the body, many kinds of data will be taken, and swarm robots have the possibility of assisting fertilization and deliverying the drug. However, it is necessary to make nano-scale robots for the application in the body, and it’s difficult for electric robots. Then we suggest swarm robots made of biomolecules. In previous research, the convenience of the molecular sensing, using the aggregation of the gold nano particle whose surface is modified with DNA strands, was indicated. By using this technology, higher sensitivity is realized. In our project, swarm robots ware made by beads a nd DNA strands, and the more practical swarm movement was set as the object of research.
