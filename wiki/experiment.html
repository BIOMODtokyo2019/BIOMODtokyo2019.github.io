Experiment 1: Aggregation of Beads with normal linker

Object: Check the activity of the different concentration of normal linker

First, we checked the activity of normal linker, whether it causes aggregation or not by for kinds of the concentration of normal linker.

Method

We prepared four different concentrations of normal linker solutions (10 µM, 1 µM, 0.1 µM, control), and added them to the solution which beads D1 and D2 were in. We incubated each solution at room temperature for 1 hour and observed them with a microscope.

Result and Discussion

Fig.1 Microscopic images of each solution after one-hour incubation. Scale bar= 80 µm.

The beads aggregated when we put normal linker into D1 and D2 solutions at any normal linker-exist samples (0.1 µM, 1 µM, and 10 µM). In contrast, the aggregation was not observed in control condition. Therefore, normal linker correctly functioned.

Experiment 2: Aggregation of Beads with AND gate linker

Object: Check the activity of AND gate linker

We tested whether the combination of two types of DNA linker work as AND logic gate.

Method

We designed AND 1 linker and AND 2 linker, which bond complementarily. Then, we prepared 4 different solutions (AND 1, AND 2, AND 1 + AND 2, control), and added them to the solution which beads D1 and D2 were in. We incubated each solution at room temperature for 10 hours and observed them with a microscope.

Result and Discussion

Fig.2 Microscopic images of each solution after incubation for ten hours. Scale bar= 40 µm.

We found that the beads aggregated when we put AND 1 and AND 2 together. The beads aggregations were partially observed in the conditions of only AND 1 or only AND 2 condition. The aggregation was not observed in control condition. Therefore, AND gate linker functioned as programmed.

Experiment 3: Amplification of normal linker

Object: Check the amplification of normal linker

We investigated the amplification of linker caused by input strand in our experimental system.

Method

We designed the DNA strands (α strand) so that we could amplify normal linker from input DNA strands (α to linker). Then, we prepared 2 conditions with 2 samples. (α 100 µM 1, α 100 µM 2, control 1, control 2). We traced the amount of fluorescence which showed the bonding of DNA with a thermal cycler (in the 45 ℃ incubation condition).

Result and Discussion

Fig.3 Fluoresence intensity of samples which include α strands (shown in green line) and samples without α strands (shown in black line).

The amount of fluorescence was clearly increased in α strand solutions compared with control ones. Therefore, PEN DNA toolbox was correctly functioned, so strand α was conversed to normal linker.

Experiment 4: Amplification of AND gate linker

Object: Check the amplification of AND gate linker and the bonding of the linkers

We checked the amplification of two kinds of AND gate linker caused by input strand in our experimental system, and the bonding of AND gate linkers.

Method

We designed two kind of DNA strands (α strand, β strand) so that we could amplify normal linker from each input DNA strands (α to linker AND 1, β to linker AND 2). Then we prepared 4 kinds of samples. (α 1 nM, β 1 nM, α 1 nM + β 1nM, control). We traced the amount of fluorescence which showed the bonding of DNA with a thermal cycler (in the 45 ℃ incubation condition).

Result and Discussion

Fig.4 The time transition of fluoresence intensity of each sample after incubation in thermal cycler. The enlarged view is also shown in the lower part of this figure.

The amount of fluorescence was clearly increased in α strand + β strand solutions compared with ones in only α strand or β strand solutions. In control solution few fluorescence was detected. Therefore, PEN DNA toolbox was correctly functioned about two strands, so strand α was conversed to linker AND 1, strand β was conversed to linker AND 2. Furthermore, created two linkers (linker AND 1, linker AND 2) bonded complementary.

Experiment 5: Amplification of normal linker and Aggregation of Beads with normal linker

Object: Check whether this series of systems works; the amplification of linker by α to linker first, and the aggregation by the linker second.
Method

We prepared two samples (α 100 nM, control) and add each sample to the solution which template (α to Linker) and beads D1, D2 were in. Then we put the system in lanes of chamber. We incubated each solution at 45 degrees, and traced them with a microscope.

Result and Discussion

In the solution strand α, beads D1 and D2 were obviously aggregated compared with in the control solution. This result showed that strand α bonded to the template and the linker was amplified. Therefore, PEN DNA toolbox was correctly functioned and strand α was conversed to normal linker. Moreover, created normal linker correctly functioned, so the beads aggregated.

Experiment 6: Amplification of strand α and normal linker and Aggregation of Beads with normal linker

Object: Find the appropriate concentration of α to linker in OR gate experiment
Method

We added Strand X (2 nM) to the 4 kinds of solution which templates (each condition; X to α, α to linker, α to α) and beads D1, D2 were in, and put the system in lanes of chamber. We incubated each solution at 45 degrees and traces them with a microscope.

Result and Discussion

Fig.7 The video of the aggregation of beads seen in the sample with 10 nM α to linker. The scale bar= 80 µm.

Fig.8 Microscopic images of each solution after incubation. Scale bar= 80 µm.

When using 10 nM template α to linker, this series of systems works and beads aggregated, but there is little aggregations when using the other concentration of template α to linker. It is found that in 100 nM, 10 nM, 1 nM and 0 nM of template α to linker, 10 nM of is the most appropriate concentration. In regard to 100 nM, too much template α to linker inhibits the amplification of α by template α to α, so template α to linker does not work well. In regard to 1 nM, too little template α to linker does not produce linker much. Note that as little as 1 nM of X strand can be amplified and observed visibly in the form of aggregation.
